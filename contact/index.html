As an educator, I aim to use biology and genetics as a way for student to explore their ideas about the natural world and understanding the interconnected nature of life.

When teaching introductory molecular biology, I draw upon my knowledge of medical genetics and genomics fields to help students build their foundational knowledge using different perspectives spanning medical, evolutionary and population biology. I also emphasize using real-world examples and historical contexts as themes with which students can engage with the themes and reflect on the current narratives of science and biology.

Pedagogical Training

I completed the requirements for the Certificate in College Teaching Program with course work in: Fundamentals in College Teaching (GS750) College Teaching & Course Design (GS755) College Teaching, Diverse Learners, and Contentious Issues (GS767) Duke Summer Academy - Best Practices in Mentoring (GS 990) I was also selected as a fellow among the inaugural cohort of Teaching on Purpose Fellows through the Kenan Institute for Ethics, which brings together faculty and graduate students across disciplines to reflect on and discuss what it means to be a good teacher of undergraduates and teaching within the university system.
