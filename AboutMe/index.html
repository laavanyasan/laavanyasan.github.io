Teaching Science

I found my interest in science as I explored the the how's and why's of the world around us, and over time I realized that I loved teaching and mentoring people in science.

I am currently finishing my PhD in Genetics & Genomics at Duke University. During graduate school, I have been involved in several teaching experiences, ranging from people who were in middle-high school to undergraduates and graduate students.

I studied Biotechnology Engineering and Molecular Biology at Anna University and University of Pennsylvania for my undergraduate and master's degrees. During these years, I realized that I enjoyed merging my interests in the biological world and solving puzzles.

Teaching dogs and dog agility

My teaching philosophy in identifying motivators and setting people up for succcess also spills into the canine world.

Among my hobbies, my dog and I are invested in competitive agility, and we are working towards our first Master Agility Championship. I love working with puppies and young dogs to train them for canine good citizenship behaviours, and also build foundational skills for dog agility as a team sport and bonding experience.
